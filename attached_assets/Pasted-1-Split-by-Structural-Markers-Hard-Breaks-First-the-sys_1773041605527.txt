1. Split by Structural Markers (Hard Breaks)

First the system looks for explicit structure in the script:

Level

Stage

Chapter

Act

Example:

Level One. The Junior Tribune.

This creates:

1 title card prompt

Example:

Text centered on black background with words Level One The Junior Tribune in white.

Then the scene generation continues.

2. Sentence Chunking

The script is then divided into small sentence groups.

Rule:

1 prompt = 2–4 sentences

Example:

Script:

You arrived at the eastern frontier eight years ago as a young officer.
The garrison at Caesarea is dusty.
The soldiers are older than you expected.

Converted to one scene:

The character walks through the dusty courtyard of a frontier garrison observing older soldiers moving slowly through the space, wide shot.
3. Scene Change Detection

A new prompt is created when any of these change:

Location

Example:

You go to Damascus to meet with the other commanders.

→ New scene

The character sits among several commanders around a large wooden war table inside a dim strategy hall, overhead view.
Action

Example:

You write a report.

→ New prompt

The character writes a military report at a wooden desk inside a dim command room lit by an oil lamp, close shot.
Emotional Shift

Example:

You begin to feel something new.
Not panic. Something quieter.

→ New prompt

The character stands alone on the fortress wall staring toward the desert horizon as evening light fades, contemplative wide shot.
Time Shift

Example:

The night before the battle.

→ New prompt

The character walks slowly through a military camp at night passing soldiers resting beside small fires, wide shot.
4. Visual Compression

Narration is converted into something that can be seen.

Example transformation:

Script:

You believe him.

This cannot be visualized directly.

So it becomes:

The character listens quietly while an older officer dismissively gestures inside a dim command room, medium shot.
5. Character Perspective Rule

Every prompt starts with:

The character

This keeps the POV consistent.

Example:

The character stands on a fortress wall watching the eastern road disappear into the desert horizon.
6. Scene Density Target

The system aims for roughly:

Script Length	Prompts
1 paragraph	2–4 prompts
1 section/level	10–18 prompts
15 minute video	120–160 prompts

Your script generated:

128 scenes

Which is ideal for AI generated story videos.

7. Final Prompt Formula

Every prompt follows this structure:

The character + action + environment + camera framing

Example:

The character writes a report at a wooden desk inside a dim military office illuminated by oil lamps, close view.
8. Title Card Insertion

Whenever a level appears:

Level Two

Insert:

Text centered on black background with words Level Two in white.

Then resume scenes.

9. What Is Removed From Script

The system removes anything non-visual:

Removed elements:

internal thoughts

abstract narration

metaphors

statistics

explanations

Converted into visual situations instead.

10. Simplified Algorithm

Pseudo-workflow:

1. detect chapter markers (Level/Stage)
2. split script into sentences
3. group sentences into 2–4 sentence blocks
4. check for scene change triggers
5. convert narration → visible actions
6. generate prompt using structure:
   character + action + environment + camera
